Transcription guidelines

Please read the guidelines before you start to do this HIT.

Punctuation/Symbols

  • Do not use any punctuation or symbols like periods or commas
  • The only punctuation marks allowed are apostrophes which are used to indicate absent letters in a contraction (with no spaces), such as don't.
  • Do not abbreviate words. For example, if you hear 'Saint' or 'Street' type it that way, do not abbreviate as 'St'.
  • Do not use dashes except to indicate false starts (e.g. p- papa). For example, 'mother in law' would be 3 separate words.
  • Do not use apostrophes to mark possessives. For example, John's car would be Johns car.

Capital Letters

    There are five instances in which capital letters are to be used:

  • The personal pronoun (I)
  • Proper nouns and adjectives
  • Names of places (cities, states, rivers, etc.): Florham Park, New Jersey, New England, the United States, Mississippi River, Rocky Mountains, Death Valley, East Tennessee, the South, Main Street, River Road, Mountain Avenue; if they say University Ave type it just that way, don't expand Ave to Avenue
  • Acronyms, such as NBC or NPR (do not put spaces in acronyms)
  • Names of companies: Charlie Browns, Texaco, American Express
  • Names of people: Ann, Jim Jones, Walt, Honey and Hon (not dear and darling)
  • Names of groups: Senate, Congress
  • Months of the year, days of the week: December, Thursday
  • God and words for God: God, Lord, Allah, Buddha
  • Holidays: Thanksgiving, Christmas, New Years Day, New Years Eve, Easter
  • Letters spoken in isolation you should capitalize, J T Jones, A M (as in the time of day)
  • False starts should be capitalized if the intended word is capitalized (Aug- August; note that each is aligned as for a word)

    • Examples of times when letter(s) should not be capitalized:

  • Following words and other words like them should not be capitalized or spaced and no hyphen should be used in them, iphone, ipad, ebay, email, ereader, ipod, itune. Here is the link for our dictionary for these kinds words.
  • The first word of the transcription should not be capitalized, unless it falls under one of the rules for capitalization listed above.
  • The first word of any sentence should not be capitalized, unless it falls under one of the rules for capitalization listed above.

Titles

  • Spell out all words: mister, doctor, junior, miss, misses, miz (for Ms.), monsignor, father.

Disfluencies

  • Filled pauses are non-speech vocalizations like 'um' and 'uh'. They are transcribed as uh, um, er, ah, eh and sometimes oh. Use only these spellings. They may also be combined to include uh um, uh uh, er uh, any combination of six filled pauses.
  • False starts, whether they are repaired or not, are indicated as such with a hyphen. These occur when the speaker stops in the middle of a word and either substitutes another word or continues with the same word. If the incomplete word is not known, indicate so with '?-'. Examples:

    • I wanna call nine 0o two sev- nine four nine 0o six hundred
    • hi I want to make a ph- phone call
    • directory assistance ple-
    • it is below the ?- blue lion

Silence

  • Mark times when the main, foreground speaker is not talking with the special # symbol. This should include 1) silences, and 2) portions of the sound clip where only a background speaker is talking.

Laughter

  • Please use 'ha' to indicate laughter in isolation. When you have laughter over speech indicate this with html tags [laugh]speech[/laugh].

Breath

  • Mark audible breath with the special $ symbol.

Digit Rules

  • All numbers are to be typed out as words
  • one eight hundred, two forty five Eighth Street, three o'clock
  • four AM, December fifth
  • The number 0 said as 'oh' is typed as 0o (the number zero followed by a lower case letter o).

Unintelligible Speech

  • When you do not have a clue as to what is being said, use 'uu'.
  • When you are not sure about a word, but have an idea of what is being said, add '?' after the word. Example: 'the big blue? lion'.
  • When you do not know if the speaker made a false start or not, use '-?' (do not confuse with '?-', see Disfluencies above).

Audio

  • Please only provide the transcript for the foreground speaker, ignoring any background noise or other speakers you may hear.

Special Note

  • Some of the speech files have already been transcribed correctly by trusted raters. If your answer is different from theirs, your HIT will be rejected.
  • Please complete the entire set of files in the HIT. HITs will not be approved if sound clips are skipped. If the speech is unintelligible, use 'uu' to indicate this. If there is no speech in the sample enter a single # symbol.
  • Please only provide the transcript for the foreground speaker, ignoring any background noise or other speakers you may hear. There are always two speakers talking to each other, and the foreground sound is always louder and clearer than back ground speaker. There may also be someone giving instructions to the two speakers. Again, do not transcript this speech but use the symbol "#".
  • Please do not do any line separation when you provide transcripts.
  • Please return the HIT if you cannot hear the audio or the audio is invalid.
  • It will normally take 2 weeks to review and pay for HITs, but it may take longer.
