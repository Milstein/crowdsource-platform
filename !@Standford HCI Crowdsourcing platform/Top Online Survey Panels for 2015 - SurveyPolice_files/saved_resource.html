          I agree. Pinecone is my favorite too but is tied with YouGov. Even though I lead an alternative lifestyle compared to many other people, i.e., do not drive, no TV, am anti-GMO, etc., I can still do many of their surveys and those $3 PayPals really do help me since I'm low-income/retired. I hate those survey companies that ask you over and over again about diseases you have been diagnosed with and all this stuff about the company you work for. I appreciate it when a survey gives you one ore more chances to explain your answers further if you feel the need and who give you enough choices, i.e., if you're not working, they give you a chance to say so instead of giving you choices only on the number of employees in your company. The others mentioned here I may have joined but never hear from, or very rarely so forget about them. I go to sign up and find I'm already on there but since I never hear from them it's like I never joined at all. I noticed that since I quit SurveySpot in frustration last year I've been getting a few more from other companies, like Harris Poll. Wish survey companies wouldn't take on other companies but would just do their own surveys, like YouGov does. I got a $100 gift card from Amazon recently from YouGov from points building up. Survey companies are all so conservative though - like some give you choices of certain politicians - I'm Green and what if I do not like either choice? Should be able to write in answers more often on many questions, say I. One of my main gripes is that even though I am willing, and DO, fill out personal profiles, this doesn't seem to help me get surveys much if at all.
