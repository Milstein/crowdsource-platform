Why #codeNepal

On April 25th, Nepal experienced a devastating earthquake that took the lives of more than 8000 people and have left thousands more injured and homeless. 
Along with world heritage sites, several schools, colleges and offices have been destroyed, leaving the Nepalese people feeling hopeless and void.


Now it is time to bring a long-term contribution after the immediate post-earthquake relief. 

The Impact in Nepal

In 3 months, CodersTrust will impact the lives of more than 1000 people in Nepal directly.
90 students will learn basic data entry skills, more than 100 will learn Front-End Web Development and approximately 1000 existing students will take part in seminars to kick-start their freelancing careers. 

The students enrolled in the Basic Data Entry training will receive the tool to start earning $ 2/3 per hour after 3/4 months of constant practice. This is a lot of money for people who are used to earn $1 a day.

The students enrolled in Front-End Web Development will be instead able to earn 10/12 $ an hour after 5 months of practice. 

We will help the students finding jobs with a dedicated team of bidders who will look for jobs on the freelance portals such as Upwork.com.



We will be focusing on both the central area of Kathmandu and the rural areas around the city, in order to impact people from different backgrounds and who have been highly affected by the earthquake.


CodersTrust needs you!

We will bring qualified mentors, project managers and top notch curriculum. However, we also need a fully equipped tent that will accommodate at least 30 people, laptops and internet access. 
We will bring our expertise and need your support to provide everything else, absolutely free of cost for the students!

Every dollar you spend will be spent to improve the lives of the Nepalese people by providing them FREE access to IT education, and seeking employment opportunities on the online freelancing market. 


CodersTrust believes in borderless access to education and the labor market.

CodersTrust (CT) is a Danish for-profit IT training and outsourcing company. Currently, CT is based in Dhaka, Bangladesh where we have reached nearly 700 talents in 10 months. 
Our mission is to change the lives of 100,000 Bangladeshis in the span of 3 years while expanding our program to other parts of the world. Our program is supported globally by DANIDA and locally we are working in a joint venture with Grameen Solutions, Bangladesh.
Well renowned entrepreneurs and individuals such Morten Lund and Douglas Rushkoff (along with many others) believe in our cause and are supporting us as advisors and board members. 
At CodersTrust, we believe in borderless access to education and the online labor market for everyone, regardless of race, gender, age and geography. 
CT envisions a world where your past does not define your future!



Where money goes

We will use the money to:

- Training room rent,

- Buy the necessary IT equipment,

- Salaries for the mentors and the bidders who will help the students finding jobs,

- Expenses like food and accommodation for the team.


2 locations

We will lead classes in two different locations - one in the city center of Kathmandu and the other one in Bhaktapur, where we will impact students from rural areas and highly affected by the earthquake.


The Team

To run such an operation, we partnered up with a team in Kathmandu that provides experience and local knowledge, key factors to successfully run #codeNepal. 

The local team will be led by Vijay Shrestha, who has held top positions in Nepal's airline industry since the last 20 years, and IT and management education entrepreneur since the last 15 years. Vijay was President of Airline Operators Association of Nepal, Board Member of Nepal Government's Civil Aviation Authority, Board of member of Management Association of Nepal, Advisor to the Mayor of Kathmandu. Started two years ago and running a cancer charity organization (self funded) for creating awareness of cancer and screening of cervical cancer among Nepalese women. Currently, Vice President of Himalaya Airlines, a Nepal-China joint venture international airline. Keen in education and development sectors besides aviation, tourism and hotel. 

Part of the team in Dhaka, Bangladesh.


Success stories from Bangladesh

We currently have 500+ students enrolled in Dhaka, Bangladesh in just 1 year of local operations.

We started from a first pilot of 20 students June 2014, improved our product around them and got ready for a full launch last February.

We have an amazing team of 25 people (of which 23 are locals) running the local CT.

After only two months of education, our students already earn $150/200 a month, with no prior IT experience.  The best case is of a student who landed a $10.000 project with an international client just two months into our education!!

Our students.


CodersTrust with UNDP in Dhaka

If you are determined enough, yesterday's impossible can be today's reality. Coderstrust, together with United Nations Development Programme - UNDP, is starting on a new project to help the most deprived segment of the country. 
We are going to provide basic IT lessons to people living in slums and try to help improve their current living standards. Amidst all the hardships, we see the glimmer of hope for a better future and that is enough to keep us going.
